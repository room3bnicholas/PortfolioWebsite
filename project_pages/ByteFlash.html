ByteFlash

Here is a more in-depth overview of my group project ByteFlash
You will find a project overview, key features, team contributions, a development summary, and a conclusion.

Overview

This project was centered around the development of an affordable, programmable games console designed to align with the UK Government’s Electronics in Schools Initiative. The primary goal was to create a tool that would engage Year 8 students (aged 12–13) and foster hands-on learning experiences with STEM concepts. The console was built with two principles in mind: accessibility for educational use and engagement through interactive gameplay.

The development process involved a team of four* members, each contributing unique skills and expertise to the project. Despite challenges such as uneven group dynamics, lack of consistent contributions from some team members, and delays in report submissions, the project successfully culminated in a functional prototype and documentation.


Key Features of the Console

  1. Game Development

    2. The console featured three core games:

    • A multiplication game to encourage mathematical problem-solving.

    • A reaction timer game to test quick thinking and reflexes.

    • A binary code memory game to introduce students to basic concepts of computer science in an engaging way.

    These games were developed by myself, Nick Lee. I also handled the coding for the main menu and ensured that the games functioned smoothly on the 1602 LCD screen.

  2. Hardware and Software Integration

    3. The console utilized a breadboard prototype to demonstrate its functionality, with the hardware setup and coding largely led by myself. Key components included an LCD screen, buttons to interact with the system, and a potentiometer for contrast adjustment. The software was designed to be modular, allowing for incremental development and testing of individual components.

  3. Educational Alignment

    4. The console was designed to align with educational goals, focusing on STEM learning through interactive gameplay. The societal impacts section of the initial report explored the device’s potential to enhance inclusivity, sustainability, and ethical considerations in education.

Development Process and Challenges

Progression: The project saw gradual and cumulative progress rather than the completion of entire subsections in a single week. For example, game development involved incremental updates, with each iteration informing subsequent improvements.

Group Dynamics: While the group initially planned to divide responsibilities equally, the breakdown in contributions from some members forced me to take on a larger share of the workload. This led to delays in report submissions and deviations from the original gantt chart timeline.

Leadership and Coordination: In spite of these challenges, I showed leadership by attending meetings consistently, structuring reports, and ensuring that the group stayed on track. Aliyah’s early contributions to documentation and project management also played a crucial role in organizing the team’s efforts.


Outcomes and Conclusion

The project successfully delivered a functional prototype of the games console, along with comprehensive documentation and reports. The final product demonstrated the feasibility of using low-cost hardware and software solutions to create engaging educational tools for students. While the group faced challenges due to uneven contributions, the final result showcased our team’s ability to adapt and deliver despite obstacles.

This project highlights the importance of collaboration, adaptability, and resilience in achieving a shared goal. Despite the challenges posed by inconsistent team contributions, the end result was a functional, educational tool that aligns with the objectives of the Electronics in Schools Initiative. The experience underscored the value of strong leadership and the necessity of clear communication to overcome obstacles in group projects.
