LCR Meter

Here is a more in-depth overview of my group project on developing an LCR Meter
You will find an introduction, team composition, key features of the project, the proposed educational impact, technical specification, and a conclusion. This is largely based on the final report I wrote for the project.

Introduction

Our pitch was that the York Metrology Systems (YMS) LCR Meter is an innovative and affordable electronic measuring instrument designed to revolutionise STEM education by inspiring young students to explore electronics and engineering. This project was conceived with the goal of providing a user-friendly, accessible tool for secondary schools that can measure inductance, capacitance, and resistance while also offering advanced features such as voltage measurement and complex impedance analysis. The YMS LCR Meter aims to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application, fostering a deeper understanding of electronics among students.

Key Features of the Meter

The YMS LCR Meter is a multi-functional device that offers a range of features tailored for educational use:

  1. Versatile Measurement Capabilities

    • The meter measures resistance, capacitance, and inductance.
    • In addition, we planned to include advanced measurements such as ESR (Equivalent Series Resistance) of capacitors, Q-factor of inductors, voltage difference, bias voltage of diodes, and complex impedance of passive components.

  2. User-Friendly Design

    • Equipped with a rotary switch for selecting the component type, making it intuitive to use.
    • Features a 1602 LCD screen that displays measurements in clear, easy-to-read format (up to four significant figures).

  3. Educational Alignment

    • Accompanied by a user guide and lesson plans to integrate the meter into classroom activities.
    • Supports hands-on learning by enabling students to explore real-world applications of passive components in circuits like amplifiers, filters, and more.


Educational Impact

The primary objective of this project was to spark interest in STEM fields among students by making electronic measurement accessible and engaging. By providing a tool that combines simplicity with advanced functionality, the YMS LCR Meter empowers educators to create dynamic and interactive lessons. The meter’s ability to measure voltage and current eliminates the need for separate voltmeters and ammeters, reducing clutter and complexity in the classroom.

The project also emphasizes sustainability by offering an affordable solution that can replace multiple instruments, thereby promoting cost-effective resource allocation in schools.


Technical Specifications

Hardware:

The meter is based on the STM32 Nucleo F303K8 microcontroller for embedded firmware development, and features a custom PCB design for compact and reliable assembly.

Software:

The planned firmware was going to be developed to handle precise measurements and display results on the LCD screen. It featured separate code blocks for different circuit configurations (RC, LR, voltage divider) ensure accurate calculations.

Unfortunately, the YMS group was not able to produce a working compilation of the code as the original board was damaged and the software lead was not present for our demonstration.

Design Considerations:

Use of various CAD software to design circuits, for case design, and for PCB layout.


Outcomes and Conclusion

The project successfully delivered a non-functional prototype of the meter board. We demonstrated a PCB based board which was limited to only measuring complex impedance due to time constraints. The YMS group also failed to showcase a functional case for the board. However, the YMS group has made a meter fit to the specification provided.

I belive that my contributions to the project as the hardware lead were successful, as I designed the core circuits and the PCB for the board, which culminated in a board suitable for demonstration if we had a microcontroller with the correct code flashed.
